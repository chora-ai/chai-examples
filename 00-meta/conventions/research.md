# Research Note Conventions

Conventions for research notes in `02-research/`.

## Section Order

Research notes follow this section order:

1. Related Projects — wikilinks to relevant project notes
2. Related Research — wikilinks to sibling research notes
3. Sub-Notes — wikilinks to child documents in the note's subfolder

A horizontal rule separates the link header from the Propagated Insights section.

4. Propagated Insights — distilled observations from daily notes and other workflows

A second horizontal rule separates Propagated Insights from body content (analysis, framing questions, resources, etc.). Not all research notes have body content — the second separator appears only when body sections exist.

## Propagated Insights

Research notes accumulate passively through daily practice and other workflows. Each research note may include a "Propagated Insights" section for distilled observations from any source — daily notes, inbox processing, triage, or other workflows.

Format: `- distilled observation ([[source-path|source label]])`

The distilled form should stand on its own without reading the source note.

## Sub-Notes

Research notes list their child documents in the Sub-Notes section. Sub-notes live in a same-named subfolder. Sub-notes omit `type` — the filesystem location expresses the parent relationship. Use `- NA` when there are no sub-notes.
